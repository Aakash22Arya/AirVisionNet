About AirVisionNet

AirVisionNet is a deep learning-based computer vision framework for estimating PM2.5, PM10, and Air Quality Index (AQI) from outdoor images captured during both daytime and nighttime. The system provides CPCB-compliant AQI estimation, geotag-based pollution hotspot mapping, historical analytics, and an interactive web dashboard.

How a prediction works
Outdoor image
You upload a day or night outdoor photo
↓
Date & time detection
Capture time is read from image metadata
↓
Automatic day/night model selection
The right vision model is chosen for lighting conditions
↓
PM2.5 & PM10 prediction
Particulate concentration is estimated from visual features
↓
AQI calculation
Converted to CPCB Air Quality Index and category
↓
Geo-tag hotspot detection
Poor-or-worse readings with location data are flagged as hotspots
↓
Analytics dashboard
Every result is saved and tracked over time
What AirVisionNet measures
PM2.5

Fine particulate matter under 2.5 microns — the main driver of respiratory health impact.

PM10

Coarser particulate matter under 10 microns, including dust and pollen.

AQI

A single CPCB-aligned score combining both, with a health category from Good to Severe.
